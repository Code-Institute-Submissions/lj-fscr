Meet the Coaches

Michael Eglon

A Strength and Conditioning coach and Applied Sport Scientist, with 5-years of experience working in the English Premier League. He is currently the First Team Rehabilitation Coach at Crystal Palace F.C.

As an average footballer growing up, Michael began to seek ways to improve his own athletic performance which led him towards pursuing a career in Sports Science. He completed his undergraduate degree in Sports and Exercise Science at Leeds Beckett University, graduating in 2014 with a first-class honour’s degree.

Whilst seeking coaching opportunities within elite-level sport, Michael gained his personal training qualifications and began working as a personal trainer. Soon after, he was able to secure a part-time voluntary role within the Crystal Palace F.C. academy delivering S&C support within the foundation and development age groups. After a season in the academy, he was offered an internship role with the first team at Crystal Palace F.C for the 2015-16 season.

After one season as an intern, Michael became the First Team Sports Scientist during the 2016-17 season. This role required him to manage the use of technology within the club, and based on the data collected, provide insights to coaching, medical, and sport science staff to improve performance and reduce injury incidence.

Alongside this position, Michael enrolled onto the Strength and Conditioning distance learning MSc programme at St Mary’s University in 2017. Shortly after, Michael became First Team Assistant Strength and Conditioning coach at Crystal Palace F.C., which required him to assist in the planning and delivery of the gym-based strength and power programme for first-team players. However, after 3-months in this role, Michael became the First Team Rehabilitation Coach at Crystal Palace F.C. following a medical department restructure. This role, which he currently possesses, requires him to plan and deliver all pitch and gym-based training for players in rehabilitation, to ensure a smooth transition to training and match-play.

In July 2020, Michael completed his MSc in Strength and Conditioning from St Mary’s University achieving a distinction. He is currently looking to publish his thesis titled: “The Influence of Physical Load and Contextual Variables on Objective and Subjective Measures of Post-match Fatigue” in the Journal of Sports Sciences.
